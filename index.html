    CARVE Resurrection
    CARVE is a model of turning quantitative data into qualitative representations. The model takes in cases and distributional data to assign each case a qualitative predicate for any kind of quantitative dimension. The resulting representation lends itself to a different kind of AI reasoning that can be done than purely quantitative data. For example, using the proprietary reasoning methods from the QRG, if a country is LARGE, it may be more likely to export raw materials. If a country has HIGH GDP, it may also have a high Human Development Index. The model was developed in the early 2000s by Praveen Paritosh, a Northwestern QRG PhD graduate. It was never seriously tested on large datasets that have become more readily available since the model's creation. This is the gap my work is trying to fill.
